Warm Amber — Human Research Story
Medium
Visual Strategy
Amber is the warmest color in the science palette — it suggests candlelight, late nights in a laboratory, a human being's sustained personal obsession. Where navy commands institutional trust, amber builds personal connection. The cream microscope circle within the amber field reads as a specimen under warm light. Concentric amber rings suggest decades of focused attention radiating outward from a single center of inquiry. This variant speaks directly to the founder story.
Caption
She started asking questions in 1999. She stopped when she built the answer.
Full Caption
Dr. Johanna Gillbro spent 20 years researching skin science. The conclusion: conventional skincare was working against the microbiome it claimed to protect. Skinome was the answer she couldn't find elsewhere.
