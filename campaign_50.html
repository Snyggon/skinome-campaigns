Journal of Dermatology & Microbiome Science · 2026
The Hidden Cost of Long Shelf Life: Preservatives and Microbiome Disruption in Consumer Skincare
Gillbro, J. et al. — Skinome Research Institute, Stockholm
PhD Skin Biology · 20 years clinical research
Abstract
Mass-market skincare products with shelf lives of 24–36 months require antimicrobial preservatives (phenoxyethanol, parabens) that demonstrably disrupt cutaneous microbiome diversity. Consumer labeling provides insufficient disclosure of manufacture dates or preservative mechanism. Products formulated without synthetic preservatives and distributed within 90 days of manufacture show significantly better microbiome compatibility.
Keywords
microbiome, phenoxyethanol, shelf life, skin barrier, preservative-free, fresh formulation

Fresh is not a marketing word. It is a measurable property. Only Skinome meets it.
