shelf·sta·ble /ˈʃelf ˌstābəl/
adjective
Able to survive 36 months in a warehouse at any temperature, any climate, without refrigeration. This is made possible by preservatives — broad-spectrum biocides designed to prevent microbial growth. Their primary purpose: supply chain convenience. Not to be confused with: good for your skin.
fresh /frɛʃ/
adjective — see also: Skinome
Produced without preservatives. Refrigerated from lab to consumer. Expires in 90 days. Effective from day one. Microbiome-safe. Harder to make. More expensive to ship. Worth it.
