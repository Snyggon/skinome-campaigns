Strategy
Sarcastic congratulations — a pattern interrupt that forces the reader to realize their "fresh" skincare is anything but. The Swedish "Grattis" (Congratulations) lands with warmth before the punchline. Rhythm builds line by line to the devastating "and called it skincare." The kicker reframes Skinome's 90-day expiry as the proof of freshness.
Confrontational Mechanism
  • Sarcasm before substance — "GRATTIS." hooks before they know it's an attack
  • Specific years make the math real and personal
  • "You called it skincare" — the final gut punch
  • Kicker reframes the limitation (90-day expiry) as superiority
Caption (English / Swedish)

EN: A 36-month shelf life doesn't happen by accident. It happens because someone chose preservatives over your skin. Skinome chose differently. Check the manufacture date on your moisturizer tonight.
