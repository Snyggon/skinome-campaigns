Strategy
Data visualization as argument. Progress bars communicate faster than copy. The deliberate asymmetry — Skinome's bar at 25% of the full bar — is visually counterintuitive: why is smaller better? The question below reframes it. The answer lands in one sentence. This is the "less is more" confrontation: restraint as proof of integrity.
Confrontational Mechanism
  • Data drives the argument — numbers feel objective, not opinionated
  • Visual asymmetry creates cognitive dissonance (short bar = better?)
  • The question puts the decision in the reader's hands
  • "The shorter one. Always." — brevity as confidence
  • Side-note copy shows optimization intent: "warehouse logistics" vs "your skin"
