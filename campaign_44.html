Issued by: Skinome Research · Stockholm, Sweden · 2026
Recall: Your current moisturizer
This notice applies to any product manufactured more than 90 days ago.
Reason for Recall
Presence of synthetic preservatives (phenoxyethanol, parabens) known to disrupt the skin microbiome. Shelf life of 24–36 months achievable only through antimicrobial additives.
Products Affected
Approximately 94% of mass-market moisturizers sold in the EU and Nordic region.
Recommended Action
Check your manufacture date. Switch to a product that expires in 90 days. Your microbiome will notice immediately.

Strategy
Official product recall notice — a form so alarming that readers stop scrolling immediately. The small print "Not a real recall" at the bottom absolves Skinome legally while the content remains factually accurate. The action box delivers the CTA without selling anything: check your manufacture date. The brand appears as a public health voice, not a commercial one.
Mechanisms
  • Recall format = highest-urgency document format; stops scroll instantly
  • "Not a real recall" small print adds dark humor and legal cover simultaneously
  • 94% affected statistic makes the problem feel universal
  • CTA ("check your manufacture date") requires no purchase — builds trust first
Caption (English / Swedish)

EN: This isn't a real recall. But it probably should be. If regulators cared about microbiome health as much as contamination, this notice would be real. Check the manufacture date on your moisturizer. Then check the preservative list.

SV: Det här är inte ett riktigt återkallande. Men det borde troligtvis vara det. Om regulatorer brydde sig om mikrobiomhälsa lika mycket som om kontaminering, skulle det här vara på riktigt. Kolla tillverkningsdatumet på din kräm. Kolla sedan listan med konserveringsmedel.
