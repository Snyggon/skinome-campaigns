Why do brands sell retinol when retinal is 11× more effective?
By Dr. Johanna Gillbro · Stockholm Dermatology
Retinol has dominated the anti-ageing market for three decades. Walk into any pharmacy and you'll find dozens of formulas promising "vitamin A results."
What they don't tell you: retinol must convert to retinal, then to retinoic acid — the form skin actually uses. Two inefficient steps. Most of the molecule never arrives.
"The serum you're paying for is working at a fraction of its potential."
Retinaldehyde (retinal) requires only one conversion step. Independent research places its efficacy at 11× that of retinol for comparable concentrations.
Why do brands keep selling retinol? It's cheaper to produce, more shelf-stable, and easier to market. Retinal requires cold-chain handling. Most brands won't bother.
